Terrain Analysis of Marion County, IN
Major Conclusions
  • The highest slopes in the county include the following locations: the Indianapolis International Airport, the surroundings of Eagle Creek Reservoir, the surroundings of Geist Reservoir, the surroundings of Fall Creek, the surroundings of Pleasant Run, and the surroundings of the White River. Besides the airport, the steepest inclines all surround a waterway in Marion County. However, with the airport containing large areas of impervious surfaces (e.g., parking lots, runways, and roads) and only being approximately ~4.67 mi northwest of the White River, the slope may have an influence on the waterway.
    • → High slopes surrounding waterways = pollution driven towards the water.
    → There are areas around the Fall Creek and Lower White River sampling locations that contain large industrial plants, which can be contributing the heavy metals we see in the PCA data.
  • Aspect, which incorporates the direction the slopes are facing, all face inwards around the waterways described above.
  • The flow direction follows the paths of the waterways, all leading to the White River in the southwest portion of the map and continuing to the next county. The flow accumulates, then, from all of the smaller waterways into the larger waterways (White River). This further supports the above information, and it gives us an idea of how pollutants can travel.
  • The Stream Power Index (STI) describes the potential erosion that occurs due to water flow at a given point of a topographic surface. As an area and slope gradient increases, the amount of water coming from upslope locations and the water's velocity increase, and therefore, so does the stream power index and risk of erosion. It is clear that the risk of erosion is highest in and near waterways.
  • Finally, the Compound Topographic Index (CTI) is correlated with soil moisture and how wet an area is (or is not). Again, the waterways, water reservoirs, and their surrounding areas have the highest CTI values, and again, corresponding with previous results above.
